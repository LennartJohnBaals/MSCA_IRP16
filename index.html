Time Series Forecasting & Explainability in Financial Markets

Website: www.digital-finance-msca.com

O Overview

This research project, conducted under the Marie Sklodowska-Curie Actions program, investigates advanced time series forecasting methods for financial markets with a strong emphasis on model interpretability and explainability.

We develop and evaluate gradient-based explainability techniques, Local Polynomial Density (LPD) estimators, and transformer-based models (BERT) for understanding and predicting financial market dynamics.

R Research Areas

Local Polynomial Density (LPD)

Non-parametric density estimation for financial return distributions and tail risk analysis.

Gradient-based Explainability

SHAP, Integrated Gradients, and attribution methods for understanding model predictions.

BERT for Finance

Transformer-based models for financial text analysis and sentiment-driven prediction.
